The five things only the agent has, captured in one context.

Sentry sees the error. Pendo sees the click. Zendesk sees the ticket. None of them see what the agent was trying to do, or how it was set up to try. Baton captures the whole attempt, not just the failure.

Intent

What the user was trying to accomplish

The agent's interpretation of the goal, in plain language. Not an event name. A sentence.

Tool call

The exact request that broke

Tool name, parameters, timing, retry pattern. The payload that hit your product.

Expected

What the agent thought would happen

The outcome the agent was planning around, captured before the call, not reconstructed after.

Observed

What actually happened

Error class, response body, and duration. The exact delta between what was expected and what came back.

Config

How the agent was set up when it broke

The configuration the call was made against: permissions, settings, integration state, at the moment of failure.

Signals are the input. Your policy decides what each one becomes.

Baton captures the same structured signal from every customer interaction. One pipeline, one shape.

Your policy layer routes each signal to wherever it should go: a ticket in your queue, an automated response, an analytics row, a product-team notification, a windowed digest, a quiet drop. You define the rules. The policy engine evaluates per signal, updated live from your console.

Where signals land is your call: Pylon, Slack, Zendesk, or your own queue. Baton is a delivery layer, not a workspace.

Rules today. Pattern clustering, novelty detection, and anomaly signals tomorrow. The capture surface stays stable. The intelligence on top grows.

Your data. Your audit trail. Your boundaries.

  • You define the signal schema. Each context category — intent, tool call, expected, observed, config — contains individual fields. You choose exactly which ones get transmitted. What you don't ask for, your customers can't send.
  • Every report is logged and inspectable. Full audit trail of what each customer agent sent, when, and in response to which interaction. Your security and compliance teams can see the data flowing through Baton at any time.
  • Customers know what they're sending. The Baton SDK surfaces the report to the customer's agent before transmission. No hidden payloads — both sides see the same structured context.
  • Scoped by product surface. Baton captures context only at the integration points you instrument. It has no access to your customer's broader agent runtime or any other part of their stack.
  • Raw PII never leaves your process. Scrubbing runs inside the SDK before any event crosses the network. Hosted observability tools collect raw payloads first and scrub at ingest. Baton inverts that.
